What is JavaScript?

JavaScript is a versatile programming language commonly used for web development. It allows you to add interactivity, communicate with servers, and manipulate the DOM (Document Object Model).

Currently, I've been messing around with JavaScript because I believe I've found something I'm starting to be passionate about...accessibility to tools through the internet and webpages. I don't like having proprietary software or things stuck behind paywalls/subscription plans like Microsoft Office tools or softwares. I'm talking about things like the Google Drive products, translation websites, draw.io, etc.

While I have an interesting opinion on how Wikipedia manages its donations/funds, and they seemingly are always "on the brink of shutting down" and need donations to survive, I think the central idea that Wikipedia is a website that is for the good of the public where anyone can access information/tools without having to pay.

Because of that idea, I've been looking into web development/not having to download things to your computer to use. As easy as it is to use Facebook, Instagram, Spotify, etc. as apps on your phone as you download them, imagine having to download/install apps on your computer the same way just to use them! I don't like that!
