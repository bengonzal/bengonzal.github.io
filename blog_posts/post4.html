New tires

Fairly recently, I went on a trip with my lovely Rose for our 2.5 years of being together and realized part of the way through the drive that the tread on my tires were VERY LOW. I didn't bother to check the tread a good time before the trip to actually change them before we hit the road, so part of the way through the trip I had to stop in a side town and pay almost $200 for a SINGLE tire change and installation! It was on the worst tire, where the tread was easily flush with the wear bars, so I had to get it changed ASAP. We were able to make the rest of the journey and back safely without anything happening to the other tire, so that's that! I got back and my dad graciously ordered and set up an appointment for me to get my tires swapped, so I'm now road ready! (hopefully)

New job?

I saved this point for discussion last because it goes a little with the reason for making this post! I had the opportunity to interview for 2 positions recently. One position was for an office coordinator at the company I work for right now, and although I thought the interview went great and I was totally qualified for the position, I didn't get a second interview :(

The other position was for a seasonal hire at Costco, and I don't know how many of you are familiar with Costco, but even the seasonal hires make a good amount more hourly than I do right now. I was grateful to be able to accept that position, and even though it is only for this upcoming busy holiday season, I am hoping that I can stay on as a full-time employee at the end if they would have me! I know the work is hard, but I would eventually like to work my way up to the corporate offices in Washington as a Software Engineer and carry out what I spent so long studying for at University! Only time will tell, but as of now, I am going to enjoy learning new skills, working my ass off, and saving money with 2 part-time jobs that hopefully ease the bills.

With those things being mentioned, I had an idea for a program that could showcase a little of my skills with working on automation tasks. Costco's scheduling system doesn't allow for importing/exporting into a digital calendar, HOWEVER -- it does show the calendar on a webpage that is (hopefully) neatly formatted and able to be scraped which I can then use to transfer to an ICS file (importing/exporting to/from a digital calendar like Google calendar or iCalendar). That is my program idea! I'm writing it down right now to be able to remember when/what I wanted to accomplish, so hopefully in the near future I am able to make a post showing off my work!
